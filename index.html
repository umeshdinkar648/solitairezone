            How to Play

            Klondike Solitaire is one of many solitaire games, which are also known as patience games in Europe. Here is an outline of the bedrock principles: You need one player, 52 French-suited cards, and a table with three zones, called tableau, foundation, and stock.

            In the tableau, you place multiple card piles with each top card revealed. The stock holds all remaining cards face-down to draw from. Now, you must combine the revealed cards tactically in order to reveal and free all cards on the table. The game is solved by placing all cards correctly sorted in the foundation.

            Each step of the game follows specific rules, which you can find in detail in our free Solitaire manual. Informative explanations of individual Solitaire terms can be found in our alphabetical Solitaire glossary.

            While it sounds easy at first, it packs a punch! Some classic Solitaire games are difficult to solve, and, rarely, one cannot be solved. As there are also countless Solitaire variations, with variations in the setup and difficulty level, we offer you plenty of custom rules.

            Multiplayer Mode

            Solitaire and multiplayer mode are not mutually exclusive: At the Solitaire Palace, you can play free Solitaire live against fellow players from around the world! At the table, everybody gets to solve the same setup to make the solution process comparable. Then, it is about who reaches the solution most effectively and fastest. Every move counts! Focus and solve the game with as few moves and stock rotations as possible!
